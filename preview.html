MEMOIR EXCERPT

I have never done life halfway. I have lived in extremes — all in or all out, thriving or unraveling, building something beautiful or watching it burn to the ground. At one point, I had eight years of sobriety, a degree, a professional career, and I was the mother I always wanted to be for my children. Then, within only a few months of relapsing, I had lost everything I had worked so hard to rebuild.

Prison did not begin my story, but it forced me to finally stop running from it. It made me confront the patterns, the pain, and the choices that kept pulling my life off course. Somewhere between recovery, reflection, and learning to code, I started to understand something I had been missing all along: survival is not the same thing as stability.

This memoir is about addiction, incarceration, motherhood, second chances, and learning how to rebuild a life with intention. It is about what happens when you stop asking how everything fell apart and start asking what it will take to build something stronger.
